Frequently asked

How does the free trial work?

Gateway is free to use for 48 hours from first launch, with every feature unlocked. A countdown badge at the top of the amp screen shows the time remaining. When the trial ends, a screen appears offering the one-time purchase.

How do I unlock Gateway permanently?

Gateway is a single one-time purchase — no subscription. You can buy at any moment during the trial by tapping the amber countdown badge at the top of the amp screen, or from the screen shown when the trial ends. Once purchased, all features stay unlocked forever.

I bought Gateway but it's asking me to pay again.

This usually happens after reinstalling or switching devices. Open the purchase screen and tap Restore Purchases — your unlock is tied to your Apple Account and will be restored at no charge. Make sure you're signed in with the same Apple Account used for the original purchase.

Do I need a TONE3000 account?

No. Gateway works fully without one. Connecting a free TONE3000 account is optional and simply lets you browse and download amp models and impulse responses from their community library directly inside the app. You can also import your own .nam and .wav files from the Files app instead.

How do I use Gateway as a plugin (AUv3)?

Gateway includes an Audio Unit (AUv3) extension. Open a host app that supports AUv3 — such as GarageBand, Logic Pro, AUM, Loopy Pro or Cubasis — and add Gateway as an effect on an audio track. The app and the plugin share one library, so any model or IR you save in the app is available in the plugin too.

I plugged in but there's no sound.

A few things to check:

  • Allow microphone/input access when prompted (or enable it in iOS Settings → Gateway). Gateway needs input access to process your audio interface or microphone.
  • Confirm your audio interface is connected and selected as the input in iOS.
  • Check the input and output gain in the app, and that the amp is powered on.
  • If you're in a host app, make sure Gateway is on an input-monitored track.
What do I need to run Gateway?

An iPhone or iPad running iOS 16 or later. For playing guitar, a wired audio interface gives the best latency and sound, but Gateway also processes the built-in microphone so you can try it without extra hardware.

What happens to my audio and data?

Your audio is processed entirely on your device and is never recorded or uploaded. Gateway collects no personal data and contains no analytics or tracking. See the Privacy Policy for the full details.
